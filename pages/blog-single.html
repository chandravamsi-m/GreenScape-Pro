5 Spring Seeds for Pollinators to Plant Right Now

Elena K. · June 18, 2026
· 5 min read
Home / Blog / 5 Spring Seeds...

Springtime is the ideal season to prepare your soil beds for the yearly cycle of pollinators. Bringing native bees, butterflies, and organic hummingbirds to your garden requires strategic plant layouts. Below are the five best seeds we recommend planting during early spring months.

1 Purple Coneflower (Echinacea purpurea)

Extremely resilient, purple coneflowers hold deep nectar reserves that butterflies love. They survive hot dry summers and return every year without replanting. Plant in areas that receive at least 6 hours of daily sunlight and watch native bees congregate on the sturdy orange seed heads.

2 Wild Bergamot (Monarda fistulosa)

Also known as Bee Balm, this native mint cousin blossoms into lavender tubes that hold rich pollen reserves, attracting native honeybees instantly. It exhibits excellent resistance to local pests and performs best in well-draining garden beds.

3 Lanceleaf Coreopsis (Coreopsis lanceolata)

Producing bright yellow, daisy-like blossoms, Lanceleaf Coreopsis is a staple for pollinator restorations. It thrives in poor, sandy soil where other plants struggle. These early-blooming seeds establish quickly and provide a vital nectar source for bees emerging in mid-spring.

4 Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta)

A vibrant summer favorite, Black-Eyed Susan seeds should be sown in early spring to guarantee a long, gold-petaled blooming window. Their dark center seed heads are highly attractive to local insect species, and they tolerate partial shade setups nicely.

5 Common Milkweed (Asclepias syriaca)

Milkweed is the absolute, exclusive host plant for Monarch butterfly larvae. By planting milkweed seeds in your borders, you provide both essential nesting leaves for caterpillars and highly aromatic nectar pods for mature butterflies.
